Understanding Addiction

Addiction is a complex condition, a brain disease that is manifested by compulsive substance use despite harmful consequences.

Get Help Now

What is Addiction?

Addiction is a chronic disease characterized by drug seeking and use that is compulsive, or difficult to control, despite harmful consequences. It can involve substances such as alcohol and drugs, or behaviors like gambling.

Common Types of Addiction

  • Alcohol Addiction
  • Drug Addiction (e.g., opioids, cocaine, marijuana)
  • Gambling Addiction
  • Internet and Technology Addiction
  • Food Addiction

Signs and Symptoms of Addiction

While specific signs can vary depending on the substance or behavior, common symptoms include:

  • Inability to stop using the substance or engaging in the behavior
  • Neglecting responsibilities and relationships
  • Increased tolerance and withdrawal symptoms
  • Continuing use despite negative consequences
  • Spending excessive time and resources on the addictive substance or behavior
  • Failed attempts to quit

Support Groups

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Learn to identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors associated with addiction.
