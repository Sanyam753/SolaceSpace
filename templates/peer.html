Understanding Peer Pressure

Peer pressure is the influence exerted by a peer group, encouraging individuals to change their attitudes, values, or behaviors.

What is Peer Pressure?

Peer pressure refers to the influence exerted by a peer group, encouraging an individual to change their attitudes, values, or behaviors to conform to the group's norms. It can be both positive and negative, affecting various aspects of a person's life.

Types of Peer Pressure

  • Direct peer pressure
  • Indirect peer pressure
  • Positive peer pressure
  • Negative peer pressure

Common Areas of Peer Pressure

  • Substance use (alcohol, drugs, tobacco)
  • Sexual activity
  • Academic performance
  • Fashion and appearance
  • Social media usage
  • Risky behaviors

Strategies to Resist Negative Peer Pressure

Develop Self-Confidence

Build strong self-esteem and confidence to stand firm in your decisions.

Choose Positive Friends

Surround yourself with friends who share your values and support your goals.

Practice Saying No

Learn and practice assertiveness techniques to confidently refuse negative influences.
