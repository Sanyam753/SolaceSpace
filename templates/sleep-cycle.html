Understanding Sleep Cycles

Sleep is essential for good health. Understanding the different stages of sleep and their importance can help improve your sleep quality.

Get Help Now

What are Sleep Cycles?

Sleep cycles are a series of stages that the brain moves through during sleep. Each cycle consists of various phases, including light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (rapid eye movement) sleep.

Stages of Sleep

  • Stage 1: Light sleep, where you drift in and out and can be awakened easily.
  • Stage 2: Eye movement stops and brain waves slow down.
  • Stage 3: Deep sleep, also known as slow-wave sleep, where it is difficult to be awakened.
  • Stage 4: REM sleep, where dreams occur and the brain is very active.

Importance of Sleep Cycles

Proper progression through sleep cycles is essential for physical and mental health. Disruptions can lead to various issues such as sleep disorders, impaired memory, and reduced immune function.
