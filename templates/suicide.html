Understanding Suicide Prevention

Suicide prevention involves addressing the complex factors that contribute to suicide risk and promoting protective factors that support mental health and well-being.

Warning Signs

  • Talking about wanting to die or kill oneself
  • Looking for ways to kill oneself
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or having no purpose
  • Talking about feeling trapped or being in unbearable pain
  • Talking about being a burden to others
  • Increasing use of alcohol or drugs
  • Acting anxious, agitated, or reckless
  • Sleeping too little or too much
  • Withdrawing or feeling isolated
  • Showing rage or talking about seeking revenge
  • Displaying extreme mood swings

Risk Factors

  • Mental disorders, particularly depression
  • Alcohol and substance abuse
  • Previous suicide attempts
  • Family history of suicide
  • Chronic pain or illness
  • Exposure to suicidal behavior
Suicide Prevention Lifeline

Suicide Prevention Strategies

Reach Out

If you're concerned about someone, reach out and show you care. Listen without judgment and offer support.

Reduce Access to Lethal Means

Remove or limit access to potentially lethal items or places, especially during times of crisis.

Seek Professional Help

Encourage individuals at risk to seek help from mental health professionals or counselors.
